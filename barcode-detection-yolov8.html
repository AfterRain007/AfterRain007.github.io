Barcode Quality Assessment Using Computer Vision (YOLOv8)

Visualization Dashboard Website Development

You Only Look Once!

This end-to-end solution was developed to automate barcode quality verification for sales cabinets using computer vision. A YOLOv8 object detection model lies at the heart of the system, trained to detect barcodes in field images submitted by sales personnel. The goal was to increase scan accuracy, reduce manual verification time, and improve compliance monitoring across distributed field teams.

Around 1,200 image samples are manually labelled using Label Studio to create the initial training dataset. During early testing, the model struggled with a high rate of false positives particularly in environments where gates or patterned objects mimicked barcode structures.

Gate Falsely Detected as a Barcode.

A new class, labeled 'Not Barcode,' was introduced to explicitly teach the model to ignore misleading patterns. As a result, model accuracy dramatically improved from ~60% to 93%, effectively eliminating the primary source of error while demonstrating robustness in real-world conditions.

Despite having no dedicated budget or high-end infrastructure, the model was initially trained using Google Colab's free-tier T4 GPU to prove the concept's feasibility before requesting further investment. And then GTX 1050 are used for the labeling pipeline to process over 4 million data entries, overcoming significant hardware limitations through optimization and task segmentation.

The results were integrated into a Power BI dashboard, allowing the Cabinet Care team to monitor scan activity, barcode status, and follow-up actions by region and date. Asset photos, scan outcomes, and field metadata are all dynamically visualized, enabling faster operational decisions and improved accountability. This solution not only automated a time-consuming process but also laid the foundation for future enhancements in field compliance analytics, proving the value of practical AI deployment in everyday operations.
