Bitcoin Sentiment Analysis

Machine Learning Deep Learning NLP AI Sentiment Analysis Python Transformers

Enough talking about bitcoin price, what about the sentiment?

This thesis investigates the performance of Temporal Fusion Transformers (TFTs) in predicting Bitcoin prices, comparing it to established models like Long-Short Term Memory (LSTM), Gated Recurrent Units (GRU), and Temporal Convolution Networks (TCN). Utilizing Volume, Sentiment, and Google Trend data as predictors, the study demonstrates that TFTs achieve superior accuracy. Furthermore, TFTs offer a significant advantage by providing insights into variable importance, addressing the "black-box" nature of other models.

Data preprocessing played a crucial role in this research. To mitigate the impact of noise and volatility, techniques such as outlier handling (using Simple Moving Average and Boxplot) and data normalization (employing First Order Differencing Transformation) were meticulously explored and implemented. The findings conclusively establish the superiority of TFTs over its predecessors. Notably, the model's explainability is highlighted by identifying Trend and linear_increase as the most influential variables in the Encoder, with Trend exhibiting a significance exceeding 50%. In the Decoder, Trend and week emerged as the dominant factors, with Trend surpassing 40% and week exceeding 10%. Moreover, the model's attention mechanism reveals a significant focus on the seven data points preceding the forecast, indicating a strategic utilization of historical information for accurate predictions.
