Bitcoin Multivariate Forecast

Time Series Forecasting Transformers Financial Analysis Deep Learning Python

This research investigates the effectiveness of Temporal Fusion Transformers (TFTs) in predicting Bitcoin prices, comparing its performance against traditional time series models such as Long-Short Term Memory (LSTM), Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs), and Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCNs). The study incorporates key variables including Volume, Sentiment, and Google Trends to enhance prediction accuracy.

Data preprocessing plays a crucial role in achieving optimal model performance. This research meticulously explores various preprocessing techniques, including outlier handling methods such as Simple Moving Average and Boxplot, as well as data normalization through First Order Differencing Transformation to address volatility.

The findings demonstrate the superiority of TFTs in Bitcoin price prediction. Unlike black-box models like LSTM and GRU, TFTs offer valuable insights into variable importance. Analysis reveals that Trend and linear_increase are the most significant variables in the Encoder, with Trend exhibiting the highest significance exceeding 50%. In the Decoder, Trend and week emerge as the most influential factors, with Trend surpassing 40% and week exceeding 10%. Furthermore, the model's attention mechanism demonstrates a clear pattern, with peak attention observed at point -7, indicating that the model effectively utilizes information from the preceding seven data points to generate accurate forecasts.

This research underscores the potential of TFTs in time series forecasting, particularly in domains requiring both high accuracy and interpretability.
